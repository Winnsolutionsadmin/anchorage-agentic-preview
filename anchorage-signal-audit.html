Honesty caveats (stated up front)

The window is the supplied corpus (Anchorage Oct 2025 to Jun 2026). Engagement is favs plus reposts plus replies. The Anchorage rows are not AI-tagged in the source, so we do NOT claim a measured AI share for Anchorage; the agentic-silence point is read from content, not a tag.

Comparisons are original-vs-original (Anchorage originals against competitor originals) so volume mix does not distort the gap.

Finding 1

The brand replies more than it speaks

Only 35.7% of Anchorage's activity is original. The majority is replies and reposts. This is an amplification feed, not an authored voice.

381 original posts over roughly eight months is a thin authored signal for a brand that wants to be known as an infrastructure leader. The reply and repost majority means the brand mostly reacts to other people's narratives instead of setting its own. The fix is not more volume. It is more original voice with a point of view.

The Synthesis

The one thing only Anchorage can own

Put the three findings together. The ownable position sits where his strongest CLEAN belief, an uncontested lane, and the empirically winning angle all overlap.

His strongest ground

Security and regulation

S2, S4, S34, S11: his highest-conviction, CLEAN-to-say beliefs. A chartered bank's home turf.

The open lane

Accountability

Everyone races on capability. No one is the voice of who is responsible when an autonomous agent gets it wrong.

What wins

The security story

The #1-performing AI post in the field is a security and accountability story, not a product brag.

“There's a thousand things to get right. There's not one. It only takes one thing to get wrong.”

Nathan McCauley, on security as discipline

The wedge: when an agent moves money, who is accountable? That question is a chartered-bank question, it is Nathan's strongest belief territory, it is uncontested, and the market already rewards it. Agentic-banking becomes the proof point that the question is live, not the headline. This is the test every campaign must pass: is it special (uncontested), unique (only a chartered bank can say it), and belief-aligned (high frequency, high conviction, CLEAN).

What Changes

What the brand does differently, starting now

  • Speak, do not just reply

    Lift original posts from a third of the feed toward a deliberate authored cadence, most of it in Nathan's voice.

  • Lead with point of view and security, not product

    The categories that win are POV and the accountability story, not feature announcements. Retire the low-yield reply-and-repost filler.

  • Run the wedge, gate the capability

    Lead every sprint on a strong CLEAN belief and the accountability question. Keep agentic as a flare. Gate all capability, partner, and volume claims to compliance.

  • Measure conversion, not volume

    Track engagement per original post and share of voice on the owned terms, not raw post count.

The bottom line

The data does not say post more. It says speak, in his voice, where a chartered bank wins.
