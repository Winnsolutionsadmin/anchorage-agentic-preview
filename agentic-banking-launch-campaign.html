00 / CAMPAIGN OBJECTIVE

Plant a category flag, not ship a product.

Campaign 1 announces an initiative and a category, opens a waitlist, and names Anchorage as the regulated trust root the agent economy will need. It does not, and must not, claim a generally available platform. A launch with a single capability over-claim is a failed launch regardless of the other numbers.

The thesis
Trust is the bottleneck

The hard problem in the agent economy is no longer model capability. It is trust. An agent that can hold and move a dollar needs a place that is funded, governed, identified, and accountable. That is a banking problem before it is an AI problem.

The move
Ask the question first

Whoever names the category's defining question owns the frame. We publish "Who Banks the Agents?" and let every competitor's wallet demo answer to it. We define the contest; rivals compete inside our terms.

The proof
Show, don't tell

The recruiting agent is itself the demonstration: a live, public, accountable agent with a spend policy of exactly zero. Every funny refusal is a screenshot-worthy lesson in Know Your Agent.

What "win" means for Campaign 1

  • Frame ownership. "Who banks the agents?" and "Agentic Banking" enter the field's vocabulary, attributed to Anchorage and to Nathan.
  • Latent demand, measured. A real waitlist of builders and institutions, ordered by signup, growing on a referral coefficient, that proves there is a market before a line of platform ships.
  • Talent pull. Inbound from infra engineers who want to build at the trust layer, triaged by the agent itself.
  • A clean launch. Zero capability over-claims, zero un-cleared partner names, zero leaked timeline. The honesty scaffold holds across every surface.
Honesty scaffold (non-negotiable). Agentic Banking is pre-launch. We tease the CATEGORY and the partnership relationship; every capability, timeline, partner-name, market-size, and regulator-posture claim is gated behind demoable substance. No Nathan-voice content ships until VOICE_SPINE reaches v1.0 and compliance signs off. Urgency is our exhortation, never a Nathan quote.

02 / THE 8-PHASE LAUNCH SUITE

The launch machine, applied to Agentic Banking.

The repeatable suite runs in order: belief tease → vision → research gap → partner → proof → launch → traction → amplify. Sprint 1 compresses phases 1 through 6 into the launch sprint; 7 and 8 are the immediate follow-on; the gated proof demo (P5) may slip out of the sprint entirely, by design.

1

Belief tease

T-14 → T-7 · stacks at W0 · X / Nathan voice

Open the frame days before anyone knows there is a launch. A short sequence of principle tweets, one every few days, that plants the question and the mental model so the reveal lands on prepared ground.

  • Tease 1 · the frame. "Everyone's optimizing how smart agents are. Almost no one's asking the question that actually gates the whole thing: when an agent moves money, who's accountable? That's not a model problem. It's a bank problem."
  • Tease 2 · the allowance. "Don't give your agent your bank account. Give it an allowance. Controls aren't the brake, they're what lets it scale without going haywire."
  • Tease 3 · KYA. "Know Your Customer has run banking for decades. The agent economy needs the same discipline for a new kind of customer. Know Your Agent."
  • Tease 4 · the bridge. "What actually lets agents act isn't a better model, it's financial rails they can be trusted on. Trust, identity, governance. And it's a banking problem."

CLEAN principleCOUNSEL on specifics Category, principle, and precondition only. No capability, date, partner, or number flips a tease to COUNSEL.

2

Vision reveal

W0 → W1 · page + Video #1 · the flag in the sand

The flag goes in the ground at embargo-lift. The landing page goes live, Nathan's reveal video fires the same hour, and the concept teases (KYA, allowance, the trust layer) frame the category as principle.

  • Video #1 · the reveal (~50s). Nathan to camera: "The hard part isn't intelligence. It's money... We're a chartered bank. So we're opening the front door today. We call it Agentic Banking."
  • Landing page hero. "Who banks the agents? ... The front door is open. Get in line."
  • Concept teases. Trust layer, Know Your Agent, the allowance model, presented as the category and the principle, never as shipping features.

3

Research gap · "Who Banks the Agents?"

W0 · at or just before launch hour · the page's intellectual spine

The paper names the gap and frames who is positioned to fill it. It is the intellectual spine the whole campaign cites, and the artifact that makes "category leadership" credible rather than asserted.

  • The named gap. The buyer-side problem most of the field skips: who vouches for the agent, and who is accountable when something goes wrong.
  • The three primitives. Know Your Agent (KYA), the allowance model, and the trust layer before the rail, each argued as principle.
  • The distribution. Linked from the page hero as the secondary CTA; cited in the press exclusive; the source for the Space topic and recap thread.

CLEAN macroVolume numbers COUNSEL Any modeled agent-traffic ceiling is a labeled destination, not current volume, and stays out of the load-bearing claims. Load-bearing stats are source-checked before publish.

4

Partner

T+2 → T+5 (W1) · partner interview · default to investor co-author

Many hands, not one bank alone. The partner beat validates the trust root from the outside. We default to an investor co-author for the launch window and hold any named-capability partner until consent is clean.

  • Default surface. A partner interview or co-author conversation (investor-voiced), framing why the regulated trust anchor matters to the rest of the stack.
  • Mastercard handling. Named launch partner of Agent Pay for Machines, never "exclusive," and only at the cleared altitude. Partner quote-tweets are held for the P8 follow-on, not launch hour, so the launch reads as Anchorage-voiced category leadership.
  • The discipline. Partner-voiced enthusiasm stays clearly theirs. Socialized interest is never presented as closed pipeline.

CLEAN relationshipPartner name COUNSEL If consent is not clean by embargo, hold the partner beat entirely for the follow-on.

5

Proof / demo

W3 → W6 if substance clears · 60-sec demo · may slip entirely

A 60-second film of an agent opening an Anchorage account, holding USD, and settling a machine payment, with the accountable-human node lit in every frame. The one thing a wallet-only demo cannot show.

  • The arc. An agent (no human at the keyboard) → Know Your Agent identity → holds USD on a segmented allowance → settles a payment within mandate → the whole loop as one clean diagram.
  • Register. Institutional-calm, no hype-reel cuts. The flatness is the message: boring, essential plumbing working exactly as it should.
  • The gate. Does not ship until the substance is real and demoable. Until then it is a storyboard held in reserve; any internal cut is watermarked CONCEPT, NOT A LIVE PRODUCT on every frame.

GATED-SUBSTANCE · hard stop The sprint launches the category without it. Every on-screen number is illustrative or real, never a stated volume or market figure.

6

Launch

W0 · the launch-hour stack · category + waitlist + bot + talent

Everything stacks in the same hour for maximum noise. The launch is an initiative, not general availability.

  • The stack, same hour: page live + waitlist opens + bot live + Video #1 + launch tweets (4 to 5) + embargo lifts + research paper published + talent announcement.
  • Launch tweet · the declaration. "We're a chartered bank, and we're building those rails. We call it Agentic Banking. The front door's open today."
  • Launch tweet · the movement. "We're hiring the people who'll build this... at the one bank that volunteered for the highest scrutiny to do it. Get in line."
  • Launch tweet · the category (optional amplifier). "Every competitor is building a piece of the agent's wallet. The real question is the one nobody's answering: who banks the agents?"

CLEAN as initiativeNOT GA The [link] resolves to the waitlist only. Bot guardrail review clears before the bot goes public.

Traction

Report what is real. Once there are numbers worth discussing, the traction beat turns momentum into a second wave of earned attention, all of it factual.

  • Waitlist size and growth curve (signups, referral coefficient, founding-cohort fill rate).
  • Demo / video views and share velocity on the cleanest principle moments.
  • Pilot or pipeline count (institutional inbound, partner conversations), reported only when real.

CLEAN · reporting Numbers are reported once they exist. No projected figure is presented as achieved.

Extend the frame through earned reach and selected paid support, once there is traction to discuss. The amplify beat reuses cleared lines; it does not generate fresh claims.

  • Partner quote-tweets. Held from launch hour, released here if consent is clean.
  • Twitter Space. "Who Banks the Agents?" debated live, held to T+7 → T+14 on a conservative go/no-go (see §04).

The waitlist hype loop, in detail

Signup-order capture

A monotonic position assigned at signup, stored immutably against the email. First-come ordering is the scarcity engine. Display can lag true count to stay clean; never inflated.

Position display

"You are #N in line," concrete, personal, screenshot-worthy, on signup and on return. No access date attached to a position.

Referral jumps the line

A unique referral link; confirmed referrals move the referrer up a fixed number of slots (e.g. 5), capped, with anti-abuse. "3 friends joined. You moved up 15 spots." The viral coefficient.

Founding-cohort identity

The first cohort gets a durable badge: ⚓ Founding Cohort. Identity over discount. The emotional hook that makes a position worth sharing.

Integrity. Dedupe by verified email, rate-limit signups per IP, invalidate referrals on bounce or fraud, never expose another user's data, position math auditable. The bot funnels everyone here, candidates, the curious, jailbreak tourists, and press, to one consistent friendly exit.
05 / PAID AMPLIFICATION

Paid is a demand engine, not a launch.

The launch is earned and owned. Paid exists to amplify what has already proven itself organically, to put fuel behind the moments the field is already responding to. We never spend to manufacture a launch that the organic signal has not earned.

Principle
Amplify the proven moment

A tweet, a video, or a paper that is already over-indexing organically gets paid distribution. The organic signal is the qualifier; paid is the multiplier. Nothing gated ever runs as paid.

Objective
Waitlist as the demand meter

Paid drives to the waitlist, the one honest conversion the campaign can claim pre-platform. Cost-per-qualified-signup is the spend-efficiency metric, not impressions.

Targeting
Builders and institutions only

Narrow to the agent-economy builder and institutional audiences. This is a category-definition spend reaching the right few, not a reach buy chasing volume.
