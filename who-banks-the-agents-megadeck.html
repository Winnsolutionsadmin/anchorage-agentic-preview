Agentic accountability starts with an anchor.

1.4

What an agent needs that no one provides.

"Banking an agent" is not a slogan. It is a specific, testable set of obligations a regulated custodian must carry for the agent's own assets. A credible answer carries all five at once, partial coverage is what already exists, and it is exactly what is insufficient.

1 · Identity — KYA
  • A known, accountable entity provably bound to a real principal
  • Not an anonymous address. A decade of Know Your Customer has no equivalent for software that moves money on its own
2 · Funded regulated wallet
  • The balance sits as a real deposit inside a chartered institution, protected, audited, qualified custody
  • A self-custodied keypair holds value; no one is obligated to the holder
3 · Programmable spend mandates
  • Limits a compliance officer can set, prove, and revoke, caps, velocity ceilings, kill-switches
  • Enforced at the institution, not suggested in the agent's prompt. Governance in the model is governance that doesn't exist
4 · Dispute, clawback & liability
  • Someone able to reverse, adjudicate, and absorb a bad transaction
  • Final-settlement rails have no undo. Sellers will not transact at scale with counterparties no one stands behind
5 · Treasury for idle balances
  • Working capital between transactions earns regulated yield under the same institution
  • Treasury is the difference between a wallet and an account
Read the five together
  • These are not the obligations of a payments API.
  • They are the obligations of a bank.
The test
"When the agent does the wrong thing with money, is there a regulated party that can claw it back and bear the liability, or only code?"

2.3

So the gap is somewhere else. It is in the funnel each launch travels.

A launch is not one post, it's an arc. Rivals push each product through a sequence of marketing-funnel phases: belief-tease → vision → research → partner → proof → launch → traction → amplify. Eight phases. The question that actually separates the field is not how many things you ship, but how far down that funnel you carry each one. That is where Anchorage's real gap lives.

Very few companies have adopted real agentic operating principles. Most have bought a chatbot and called it transformation. Incoming work is classified, prioritized, and routed toward the right human owner by an agent, not a person watching a queue. Blocked status surfaces itself. The dispatcher seat disappears, and nothing waits to be noticed.

Principle 02

synthesis across teams · system of record
Principle 03

Self-restarting loops

Work does not die when a step finishes or fails. The loop re-reads its own state and picks the next move, again and again, until the goal is met. The "Ralph Wiggum" continuous-loop pattern: relentlessly simple, and it does not get tired or forget.
