The Spine

Who banks the agents?

Every competitor is building a piece of the agent's wallet. Anchorage is the only one that can be the agent's bank.

As AI agents become economic actors, the thing that lets them act is regulated financial rails: a place to hold value, a way to move it, an identity for the agent itself, and controls that make all of it auditable. That is the trust layer for autonomous finance, and it is a bank problem before it is an AI problem. The load-bearing qualifier is "regulated," and Anchorage is the only fully operational federally chartered bank making the claim. That moat needs no forward-looking statement to be true.

This campaign turns one narrative into motion. Rather than a single launch, the work runs as a sequence of focused sprints, each built on the same engine, each owning one pillar of the story. Sprint 1 is live now. The sprints behind it are mapped here as proposed placeholders, so the launch reads as the first move in a campaign, not a one-off.

The one rule that governs everything

Every loud moment is backed by a real thing. The category, the initiative, the partner relationships, and the waitlist are real and can be teased now. The platform as generally available, named capabilities, and volume numbers are not, and stay gated. We lead on the layer where being early and chartered is the whole advantage, and gate every capability behind demoable substance.

The Engine

The eight-phase suite every sprint runs.

One reusable launch machine. Each sprint instantiates it: a sprint compresses phases one through six into the launch window, then traction and amplify follow immediately on real numbers.

  • Belief tease

    Open the frame in Nathan's voice before the flag goes up.

  • Vision reveal

    The loud surface: the landing page and the founder video that name the line.

  • Research gap

    The principle-grade paper that gives press and analysts a citeable artifact.

  • Partner

    The relationship teased at category altitude, never a capability claim.

  • Proof / demo

    Shipped only when the substance gate clears; otherwise held to the follow-on.

  • Launch

    The category and the waitlist open. Not "the platform is generally available."

  • Traction

    First numbers: waitlist size, demo views, pilots. The immediate follow-on.

  • Amplify

    Media hit, partner quote-tweets, recap. Extends the cycle.

The immediate next step is to turn this suite into a reusable protocol so every future rollout runs the full machine, with goal-tagging and the clean-versus-counsel gate baked in. Each sprint below is the same engine pointed at a different pillar.

How It Runs

The cadence, the discipline, the ask.

A sprint is three to four weeks of preparation, one week of execution, roughly one per month, overlapping once the machine is oiled. The next sprint's prep starts around day ten of the current one.

The two disciplines that govern the campaign

The subtraction gate. The plan is a map, not a license to build everything at once on a pre-launch platform. Pick the in-roadmap spine plus one or two beyond-roadmap bets, and park the rest behind a capacity gate.

Nothing announces ahead of demoable substance. Lead the category, which costs zero product substance, and gate every capability, timeline, partner name, and volume number. The partner-named gaps are exactly what not to announce.

The standing gate on every line

Clean means principle-safe in Nathan's voice, subject to voice review. Counsel means it routes to compliance before any external use. For a federally chartered bank, that gate is permanent, and no Nathan-voice content ships until the voice spine reaches v1.0 and compliance signs off.
