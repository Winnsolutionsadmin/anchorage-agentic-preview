01 · The launch machine

The 8-phase launch suite

Sprint 1 compresses Phases 1 through 6 into the launch sprint. Phases 7 and 8 are the immediate follow-on, gated on real numbers that do not exist yet. Lead on the layer where being early and chartered is the whole advantage; tease partnership at competitor altitude; announce capability only as substance lands.

02 · Spans the whole sprint

The parallel talent, waitlist, and bot lane

The framework under-weights this, so we elevate it. It runs across every phase: it makes recruiting a marketing surface, turns attention into an ordered audience, and proves Anchorage builds agentically without a single forward capability claim.

Parallel lane · P2 / P6

Talent brand + waitlist + the agentic bot, running together

Each element drives the next: the bot is the fun front door, the front door feeds the waitlist, the waitlist measures the latent demand, and the talent push proves the initiative is real and resourced.

Talent announcement + employer brand

Goal: make Anchorage the destination for engineers who want to build agentic financial infrastructure, and make the hiring push read as proof the initiative is serious. Phase: P2 into P6.

The waitlist (the hype loop)

Goal: convert attention into a captured, ordered, ownable audience. Signup order is public-ish status, referral jumps the line, earliest adopters get a founding-cohort identity. Phase: P6 into P7.

The agentic hiring / triage bot

Goal: roughly 75% virality, 25% pipeline. A shareable agent that triages candidates and turns jailbreak attempts into on-brand, screenshot-worthy refusals that each carry the accountability message. Phase: P1 / P2.

The guardrails (the honest part)

Goal: the bot's own accountability demonstrates the thesis. It leaks no roadmap, dates, partner names, or capability claims. A curated, compliance-reviewed refusal set, not freewheeling generation. Phase: compliance gate before launch.

Follow-onP5 / P7 / P8

Hard dependencies (the gates that block everything)

  • Voice spine v1.0 + compliance sign-off blocks all Nathan-voice copy: videos, tweets, and page voice.
  • Source-check the load-bearing stats blocks the research paper and any number on the page.
  • Compliance review of the bot's refusal and guardrail set blocks the bot launch.
  • The substance gate blocks the demo (P5) and every capability claim. P5 may slip entirely to the follow-on, and that is fine; the sprint is designed to launch the category without it.
  • Partner consent blocks the partner name in the interview.
05 · The scoreboard

Top 3 sprint goals + KPIs

Three goals, each with a measurable signal. The honesty KPI is load-bearing: a launch with a single capability over-claim is a failed launch regardless of the other numbers.

1

Own the category publicly

Anchorage is established as the chartered bank building agentic banking, the one asking who banks the agents.

KPI3+ tier-1 press pickups using the category framing within 14 days; "Who Banks the Agents?", Know Your Agent, and "give your agent an allowance" quoted back externally.
2

Capture an ordered, ownable audience

The waitlist turns launch attention into a captured, ordered audience Anchorage owns.

KPIWaitlist signups above target (proposed stretch: 2,500 in 30 days) with a healthy builder and institution mix, sustained signup velocity past launch week, and measurable referral-driven signups (the viral coefficient).
3

Prove it is real without claiming a shipped platform

The initiative reads as real and resourced, with no over-claim about what Armada does today.

KPIQualified talent inbound above target; bot interactions shared organically (jailbreak-caveat reshares); and zero counsel or gated-substance leaks across all launch surfaces (the honesty KPI).
