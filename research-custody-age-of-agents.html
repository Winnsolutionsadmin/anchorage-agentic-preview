Anchorage Research · Paper 04

Custody Must Be Usable in the Age of Agents

Give the agent an allowance, not the keys.

An Anchorage research paper. Pillar 4 — Anchorage products (custody, agentic mode). Prepared for Nathan McCauley, Co-Founder and CEO, Anchorage Digital.

Executive Summary

For a decade the custody argument has been about temperature and ownership: cold storage versus hot wallets, self-custody versus a third party. Autonomous agents break that frame. An agent cannot operate from cold storage, and handing it the private keys is not custody, it is abdication. The institutional requirement for the agent era is custody that is usable, an arrangement where an agent can transact within governed, scoped, recoverable limits while the institution retains control. The right metaphor is not a vault and not a hot wallet. It is an allowance.

Key Finding

Autonomous agents require custody that is simultaneously usable and controlled. Neither cold storage nor key handover meets that bar. Segmented, governed, recoverable custody does.

01

The institutional market already chose regulated custody

The flows settle the old debate. Institutional capital moved decisively toward regulated, third-party custody, not self-custody, as it entered crypto at scale. BlackRock named Anchorage Digital Bank an eligible custodian for its spot crypto exchange-traded products, extending a relationship that already custodied its tokenized BUIDL fund. [1] The asset manager 3iQ selected Anchorage to custody and run infrastructure for six exchange-traded products listed on the Toronto Stock Exchange. [2] Across the market, regulated custody, not key self-management, is the institutional default, and the OCC affirmed in 2025 that national banks may custody crypto and removed the supervisory non-objection gate. [3]

The self-custody maximalist position, that the only safe key is your own, does not survive contact with fiduciary duty. An institution acting for clients needs recoverability, auditability, and governance that a single private key cannot provide.

02

Agents change the requirement from storage to usability

A custodied asset that an agent cannot touch is useless to the agent economy. But an agent holding the keys is an uninsurable, ungovernable risk. The resolution is custody designed for delegated, bounded use: the agent receives a scoped allowance, not the account; spending and action limits are enforced at the custody layer; every action is logged; and the institution can revoke or recover at any time.

This is the natural extension of Nathan McCauley's framing that assets should not be defined by their temperature. The relevant property is not hot or cold. It is whether the asset can be put to work under controls the institution actually holds. For an agent, that means an allowance model: enough authority to act, never enough to run away with the account.

Key Finding

The agent era reframes custody from a storage question to a usability-under-control question. The product is a governed allowance, not a vault and not a private key.

03

Controls are the on-ramp

The instinct in a regulated institution is to treat controls as a brake on innovation. The agent economy inverts that. An agent that no institution can govern is an agent no institution will fund. The controls, scoped permissions, enforced limits, full audit trail, recoverability, are precisely what make it safe to let an agent act on real capital. They are the on-ramp, not the brake. The same logic that brought institutional capital into custodied crypto, that capital follows control, will govern which agents are allowed near institutional balance sheets.

04

The position: usable custody, from a chartered bank

The institutional custody product for the agent era is segmented, governed, recoverable custody operated by a federally chartered trust bank, exposing agents to a scoped allowance rather than the keys. A chartered bank can offer what neither self-custody nor an unregulated wallet can: the combination of usability, governance, recoverability, and a regulator behind it. Custody stops being a place assets sit and becomes the control surface through which autonomous actors are allowed to operate. That is the version of custody the next phase requires.

The position

The question is no longer where the keys sit. It is how an autonomous actor can use assets under governed, recoverable controls.
